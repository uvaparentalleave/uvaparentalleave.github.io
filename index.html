ACTION FOR PARENTAL LEAVE

On June 27th, Governor Northam issued Executive Order 12 to provide state employees with eight weeks of paid parental leave. This policy soundly aligns with our academic community’s stated commitment to employee excellence, talent attraction and retention, diversity, inclusion, and maintaining an environment free of discrimination. However, employees remain uncertain as to whether or not our administration will adopt the order. There are three key reasons this policy is good for UVA, as it is for the state of Virginia:

  1. Paid parental leave creates a more equitable work environment, as women are the breadwinners in more than 40% of American families, and women with access to paid family leave are 40% more likely to return to the workforce.
  2. Paid parental leave policies align with American values, with 82% of Americans supporting paid maternity leave and 69% supporting paid paternity leave.
  3. A formal and meaningful policy supports the health and wellbeing of University stakeholders (employees) and their families. As a result, the University will not only attract, but more importantly retain talent within our community, reducing the need for excess resource allocation towards recruitment and hiring.
